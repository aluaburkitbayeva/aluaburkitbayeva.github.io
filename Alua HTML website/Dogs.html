How Dogs Help Release Stress

Having a dog encourages communication between partners, as they coordinate tasks caring for the dog. This shared responsibility can enhance teamwork, and promote a sense of togethernesses.

Owning a dog teaches children about responsibility as they help care for the pet by feeding, grooming and exercising it. This also fosters empathy, as kids learn to understand and meet the needs of another living being.

People with dogs tend to have lower blood pressure and are less likely to develop heart disease. Just playing with a dog helps to raise levels of the feel-good brain chemicals oxytocin and dopamine, creating positive feelings.
